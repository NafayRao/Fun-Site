The Rapper and Drug Addict.

Snoop Dogg, born Calvin Cordozar Broadus Jr. on October 20, 1971, is an iconic American rapper, singer, songwriter, actor, and media personality. Emerging in the early 1990s under the mentorship of Dr. Dre, Snoop Dogg gained immense popularity with his debut album "Doggystyle," which included hits like "Gin and Juice" and "Who Am I (What's My Name)?" Known for his laid-back, smooth West Coast rap style, Snoop has consistently reinvented himself over the decades. Beyond music, he has ventured into acting, reality TV, entrepreneurship, and even cooking shows, often collaborating with Martha Stewart. His distinctive voice, charismatic personality, and influence on hip-hop culture have solidified his status as a legendary figure in the entertainment industry.

Biographies

  • Calvin Cordozar Broadus Jr., better known by his stage name Snoop Dogg, was born on October 20, 1971, in Long Beach, California.
  • Raised in a musically inclined family, Snoop developed a passion for music at an early age.
  • Despite facing challenges during his teenage years, including brushes with the law, his talent and charisma set him on a path to success.
  • Snoop Dogg's career took off when he was discovered by legendary producer Dr. Dre in the early 1990s.
  • His feature on Dre's debut solo album, "The Chronic" (1992), catapulted him to fame.
  • The collaboration showcased Snoop's distinctive laid-back flow and unique vocal style, making him an instant sensation.
